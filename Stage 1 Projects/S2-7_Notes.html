2.7 How to Solve Problems

Understanding a Computatiional Problem

  • We will look at specific problems and try to draw general conclusions about how to solve problems.
  • The first step is to make sure you understand the problem.
  • We can also search the web for examples of how other have solved the problem.
  • We need to understand Inputs, Outputs, and the relationship between them.
  • Example..."Given your birthday and the current date calculate your age in days."

Step 0...Don't Panic

Step 1...Inputs

  • What are the inputs?
  • From the above example the Inputs are 2 dates.
  • The second date must be before the first date.
  • The dates should be based on the Gregorian calendar.
  • Code Example...def daysBetweenDates(year1,month1,day1,year2,month2,day2).

Step 2...Outputs

  • There are two ways to product Outputs...Print or Return.
  • Printing output simply prints to the consol (or other source).
  • Returning output provides a value that you can do computations with.

Step 3...Solve the Problem

  • Start by working out some examples.
  • Focus on systematically solving the problem as a human.
  • Don't jump into coding too quickly.

Step 4...Simple Mechanical Solution

  • Don't optimize your code too quickly.
  • Code something simple and correct.
  • Making progress is better than perfect code.
  • Write small bits of code, test them, and know what they do.

Step 5...Incremental Development and Testing

  • Solve the problem incrementally.
  • Write Unit Tests to qualify each increment.
