2.1 Introduction to Serious Programming

Computer Science

Computer Science attempts to solve problems by breaking them into smaller pieces and then precisely and mechanically identifying the steps to solve each piece. These are then executed by a computer.

Computers and Computer Programs

Computers can't do anything without a program, but can be programmed to do any computation. Programs tell computers what tasks to perform and in what order to perform them.

Programs represent a precise sequence of steps for the computer to follow. Computations (tasks) are performed by the computer at each step until all steps have been satisfied.

Interpreters are programs that the computer uses to interpret the language used by other programs:

  • Interpreters are specific to languages (like Python).
  • The interpreter follows the instructions in your code.
  • It does that by following the instructions in it's code.
  • All of this can be run using your web browser.

Natural Language Ambiguity

We can't use spoken languages like English to write computer programs due to Natural Language Ambiguity:

  • Many people can interpret the same language different ways.
  • Computers need a single interpretation.
  • Verbosity - Too many words.
  • Computers need efficient language.
  • Computer languages have unique grammer.
  • The logic of languages is based on Backus Naur Form.
